What is EMIT?

"EMIT is a hyperspectral visible/shortwave infrared sensor designed to investigate Earth's surface mineral dust sources. Its primary objective is to improve climate models by accurately accounting for mineral dust, helping us better understand the composition of Earth's surface and how mineral dust affects global climate change. EMIT works by collecting spectral data from the Earth's surface to identify different minerals. These spectral data can provide information about the mineral composition of the surface, as different minerals have unique features in the spectrum. This data can be used to track the sources of mineral dust, understand their distribution and movement, and further assess their impact on the atmospheric and climate systems."

The Impact of EMIT on Our Lives

Dust sources with higher average iron oxide content are more likely to contribute to climate warming. Mineral dust in the atmosphere is a crucial component of the Earth's system. Winds transport dust from desert regions into the atmosphere, and these minerals absorb sunlight, potentially influencing the Earth's temperature, whether it's warming or cooling. If we can more accurately understand the sources of dust and the properties and distribution of minerals, the models we develop will provide better information for climate change mitigation and adaptation plans
